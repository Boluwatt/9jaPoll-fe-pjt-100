The Voice of the Masses in Politics

By: Olawunmi Gbarada

7th july 2019

Last year, President Buhari signed into law an Act that recognised June 12th as Democracy Day instead of the May 27th chosen by the military to formally hand over power to the civilaian government in 1999. There were however, mixed reactions in 2020, when June 12 was pronounced as Democracy as there were doubt if the gesture was actually meant to translate to good governance or bring about dividends of democracy to Nigerians. Eminent Nigerians like former External Affair Minister, Professor Bolaji Akinyemi and Chairman of National Democratic Coalition (NADECO), Chief Ayo Opadokun among others, made a strong case for poverty reductionand good governance, saying this was only on that premises the purpose for with June 12 was recognised as Democracy Day, would be achieved.
